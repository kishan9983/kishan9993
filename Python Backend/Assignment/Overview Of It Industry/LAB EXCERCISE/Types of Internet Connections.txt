1. Broadband (DSL / Cable)

Description:
Broadband is a high-speed internet connection that is always on. It includes DSL (Digital Subscriber Line) and cable internet.

Pros:

Always connected (no dial-up needed)

Relatively fast for general usage

Widely available in urban areas

Can support multiple devices

Cons:

Speed can be affected by network congestion

Limited coverage in rural areas

Cable connections may experience downtime during maintenance

2. Fiber Optic Internet

Description:
Fiber uses light signals through glass fibers to deliver extremely high-speed internet.

Pros:

Very high speeds (up to 1 Gbps or more)

Reliable and stable connection

Low latency, great for gaming and video calls

Supports high data usage

Cons:

Expensive installation and monthly cost

Limited availability (mostly urban areas)

Installation may require professional setup

3. Satellite Internet

Description:
Internet is delivered via satellites orbiting the Earth. Suitable for remote areas.

Pros:

Available almost anywhere, even in remote regions

Good for areas without wired infrastructure

Cons:

High latency (slow response time)

Weather can affect connection quality

Data caps may apply, limiting usage

Expensive compared to other options

4. Mobile / Cellular Internet (3G, 4G, 5G)

Description:
Internet provided via cellular networks to smartphones, dongles, or mobile hotspots.

Pros:

Wireless and portable

High-speed options available (4G/5G)

Easy to set up

Cons:

Speed depends on network coverage and congestion

Data limits may apply

Signal can be weak in rural or indoor areas
